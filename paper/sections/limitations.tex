\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

This is a controlled mechanism paper with a CPU-light CartPole-v1 benchmark tier. The main environment is synthetic, one-dimensional, and designed to expose the score-utility-support relation cleanly. The learned policy is DT-style but small; it is not a large Transformer and is not intended to establish D4RL, MuJoCo, Atari, or deployment-scale benchmark performance. The support estimator is simple, based on empirical return quantiles, action likelihood, or action-imbalance diagnostics. The pilot-calibrated repair assumes access to a small number of real-utility labels, which may be expensive or unavailable in realistic deployments. The oracle selector is only a diagnostic upper bound.

The repair results should not be read as a universal prescription. In this run, likelihood and pilot calibration recover real utility under the out-of-support target, while support calibration and conservative gating mostly act by lowering target ambition and risk. In CartPole-v1, the support-aware and conservative selectors repair a deliberately attackable short-horizon proxy, but this does not imply that the same simple diagnostics are sufficient for high-dimensional offline RL. Different environments could require different support estimators, uncertainty estimates, or conservative objectives. The paper therefore supports the mechanism claim and the measured synthetic and CartPole-v1 repairs, not broad benchmark conclusions.
